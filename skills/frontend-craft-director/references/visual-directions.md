# Visual Directions

Choose one direction per task unless the user explicitly wants something hybrid.

## 1. Product Crisp

Use when:
- SaaS
- internal tools
- search and workflow products
- feature-heavy apps that need clarity first

Signals:
- strong grid alignment
- restrained color accents
- medium border radius
- thin borders, selective shadows
- clean sans typography

Avoid:
- decorative gradients everywhere
- oversized hero sections in utility-heavy products
- too many badge colors competing at once

## 2. Editorial Calm

Use when:
- reading products
- research tools
- content-first experiences
- interfaces that need trust and focus

Signals:
- strong typography hierarchy
- generous whitespace
- muted warm or neutral background treatment
- lower UI chrome, higher content emphasis

Avoid:
- dense control bars above the fold
- excessive card nesting
- loud UI treatment around long-form content

## 3. Data Dense

Use when:
- dashboards
- analytics
- monitoring
- operations consoles

Signals:
- tighter spacing but still legible
- high-contrast labels and values
- clear grouping of filters and outputs
- repeatable panel logic

Avoid:
- oversized decorative hero areas
- inconsistent chart containers
- hiding primary metrics inside tabs or carousels

## 4. Premium Tactile

Use when:
- high-end consumer products
- lifestyle brands
- booking, hospitality, or curated commerce

Signals:
- richer surface depth
- more deliberate type pairing
- warmer palette
- larger imagery or hero moments
- subtle texture or light effects

Avoid:
- generic glass everywhere
- saturated neon accents without product fit
- too many material effects at once

## 5. Playful Modular

Use when:
- early-stage startups
- creator tools
- youth-facing products
- products that benefit from visible energy

Signals:
- stronger color blocking
- more assertive section contrast
- friendlier geometry
- expressive but simple motion

Avoid:
- mixing playful structure with corporate microcopy
- too many unrelated accent colors
- novelty that weakens readability

## 6. Technical Dark

Use when:
- developer tools
- AI workbenches
- terminal-adjacent products
- products where long sessions in darker environments matter

Signals:
- deep neutral surfaces
- restrained accent palette
- strong code/data emphasis
- sharp contrast around active regions

Avoid:
- low-contrast gray-on-gray text
- color glow replacing hierarchy
- every surface using the same darkness

## Selection Shortcut

- If content and reading quality matter most: `Editorial Calm`
- If clarity and product trust matter most: `Product Crisp`
- If metrics and scanning matter most: `Data Dense`
- If conversion and brand feel matter most: `Premium Tactile`
- If energy and distinctiveness matter most: `Playful Modular`
- If tool depth and focus matter most: `Technical Dark`
